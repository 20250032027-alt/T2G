Coconut Sugar & Health

T2G Coco Sugar has a glycemic index (GI) of 35, which is considered low. This means it raises blood sugar levels slowly compared to refined white sugar (GI ~65) or even brown sugar. It is a smarter choice for diabetics and anyone following a low-GI diet.
Foods with a GI of 55 and above are generally not recommended for diabetics. T2G Coco Sugar, with a GI of only 35, is considered safe for diabetics to consume in moderation. However, we always recommend consulting your healthcare professional before making dietary changes.
Coconut sugar is produced from coconut sap and is rich in naturally occurring minerals, vitamins, and amino acids. In contrast, muscovado is made from sugar cane and undergoes more refining, offering less nutritional value. Coconut sugar also has a significantly lower glycemic index than muscovado sugar.
Coconut syrup comes from nutrient-rich coconut sap, while agave syrup is refined from the agave cactus plant. While both are natural sweeteners, agave syrup has a significantly higher fructose content compared to coconut syrup. High fructose intake can be problematic for metabolic health — making coco syrup the better choice for most people.

Virgin Coconut Oil

Virgin Coconut Oil (VCO) is extracted from fresh coconut meat through a centrifuge or traditional fermentation process to separate the oil, or by cold-pressing dried coconut. With less refinement, you get more of the natural coconut flavor, aroma, and nutrients — including beneficial MCTs. Regular coconut oil, on the other hand, is refined, bleached, and deodorized (RBD) using solvents that maximize extraction but strip away flavor and nutrients.
